biography

Lily's Puff first project was born from the union of Marco Fabro and Chiara Barichello, in an essential piano-voice sound dimension. The demo turns out to be very intense due to the emotional charge it manages to convey, and soon evolves into "Domino" (1999), a work with which the collaboration with Paolo Corberi begins, who signs a song and intervenes in the arrangements of some pieces. It is a work full of nuances capable of transmitting Marco's emotional charge, supported by Chiara's incessant piano and Paolo's arrangements, which move between sounds and noises. In 2002 the mini CD "The Raven, the Thirst and the Pendulum" was published on Nail Records, a concept that reflects the new dimension into which the group has entered, the theatrical one. Live performances are enriched with texts written by Paolo Zuttioni and recited by Francesca Martinelli. The group transforms and Simone Sari replaces Chiara on the piano, and in 2003 an unpublished piece of theirs was published in the Rosa Selvaggia fanzine, "Reflecting Eyes", where the western rhythm of the guitar is intertwined with the classical melody of the piano. It is only a transition towards the sounds of "Crashing Diamond" (2004), a much denser work than the previous ones; more varied atmospheres and new collaborations: Riccardo Vatovec (drums), Silvia Turco (piano and accordion), and Virginia Boemo (vocals).
The band achieves a fine balance, resulting in a more refined and distinctive product. In fact, the reviews of the album are very good. The desire to experiment with new sound situations and venture into instrumental paths that are still new to the band emerges more and more, and materializes in the involvement of Renzo Fanutti, already alongside Paolo in other projects. Renzo's electronic-surreal streak goes well with the band's nature, and the result is "Heaven Frowns" (2007), a record that will leave fans astonished: the melodic, rhythmic and passionate charge it transmits is impressive; electronic and acoustic sounds merge in a union with an increasingly visionary flavor. Here too there is no shortage of guests, and the voice of Elisabetta Mauro is added, which embellishes a couple of tracks.
A period of further research follows; Simone moves away from the band, while another musician approaches the experimental paths that have never ceased to interest the band, Mirko Cisilino, a trumpeter introduced among other things by Simone himself.
His contribution opens new musical and melodic horizons, which intertwine with those already known to the protagonists of the group. Lily's Puff thus take the path of electro-acoustic research with ever greater conviction.
The result is "Tutum" (2014), a work that testifies to a further evolution of the band's sounds, determined to always go further in the search for the unusual and the unconventional.


line up


Marco Fabro (aka Markodarko)

The original founder (with Chiara Barichello) sings and writes lyrics

Renzo Fanutti (aka RezFx)

In the band from the album Heaven Frowns (2007), writes songs, lyrics, plays acoustic, electric and virtual instruments

Paolo Corberi (aka PaulTriglia)

Involved in the band (from the beginning actually) writes songs, lyrics, plays acoustic, electric and virtual instruments

Mirko Cisilino (aka Circomisilino)

In 2014 the album Tùtùm, was out, which involved him (trumpets, loops, fx) in all the tracks and few dates live
